Why computer use matters now

Agents are moving from talk to action

Agentic AI is defined by taking real actions — like booking, navigating, and completing workflows — not just generating text, according to recent analysis from MIT News.

Big players are investing in computer use

Google has made computer use a first-class capability in Gemini 3.5 Flash, highlighting how valuable real browser control has become for AI agents.

Cost and safety are emerging constraints

Security research has shown that naive agent designs can be fragile. Reuse, isolation, and intentional design matter as agents scale.
